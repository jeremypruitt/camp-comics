# Batch-generate + swipe-review replaces the panel-loop UX

The per-panel "tap → wait → tap" loop (ADR-0003, slices 8–14) is replaced wholesale by a two-phase batch generator feeding a single full-screen **review stack**. Phase 1 generates panel 1 alone and blocks until the operator accepts a candidate; Accept on panel 1 transitions into phase 2, which enqueues panels 2..15 + cover into a [[Generation queue]] worker pool that pre-decrements the per-comic budget and lands results into per-panel galleries as they come in. The operator works the resulting stack with Tinder-style gestures — swipe right = Accept, swipe left = [[Re-roll]] (append to gallery, newest on top), swipe up/down = cycle through this panel's gallery (zero API calls), long-press = [[Re-prompt]] (shared-addendum editor whose result lands in the gallery like a Re-roll). The stack is strictly story-ordered (panel 2, 3, …, 15, cover); a placeholder card with spinner renders at the head while the head's underlying generation is in flight or [[Throttled]]; behind-head in-flight panels are invisible. The review surface must be a full-screen NavigationStack root — never a sheet — because swipe-down on a sheet dismisses it, colliding with gallery navigation.

The motivation is dominated by attended-tap cost. Under the panel-loop, finalizing one 15-panel comic took ~20 minutes of attended button taps: tap Generate, wait ~5s, tap Accept, advance, repeat 16 times. Under the swipe model the operator hits one "Start campaign" CTA after the QA gate, walks away, and returns to a stack that has been pre-populating since panel 1 was accepted — the unattended generation time is the bottleneck, not their fingers. The mental model also shifts: a panel is no longer a thing you patiently iterate one at a time, it's a card you decide on in a glance. This same simplification scales — a future 50-panel template under the panel-loop would have been hopeless; under the swipe model it's just a longer stack with the same gestures. The redesign obsoletes much of the slice 11a–c–d work: the explicit nav row, the per-panel header card, the buttons-not-gestures action set, the in-line filmstrip — all replaced by the swipe surface plus the existing [[Panel grid]] sheet from slice 11d.

**Continuity is anchored on panel 1, not chained panel-to-panel.** Every panel from 2 through 15 and the cover takes `panel_01.png` as its continuity reference; no panel references its immediate predecessor anymore. This supersedes the "most recent accepted panel" rule from ADR-0002 and is the load-bearing simplification that makes the batch shape work — without it, panel 7 would block on panel 6 which would block on panel 5, sequentializing the whole queue. The reason the chained model is dropped (and not just relaxed for batch convenience) is that field experience showed it acting like a game of telephone — each generation drifted slightly from its anchor, drift compounded across panels, and the late-comic look diverged from panel 1's establishing style. Anchoring everything on panel 1 keeps the comic visually consistent end-to-end and turns out to be both simpler AND more stable than chaining. The cover stays at `[photo, hero]` only (no continuity reference) because its framing is structurally different from any interior panel and the hero card already supplies costume continuity. The `PanelSpec.reference_panel` YAML override is retained as an escape hatch for special cases. The single sharp edge introduced: re-rolling panel 1 after downstream panels have been accepted leaves them anchored on the old panel 1 — a warn-only confirm fires on Accept-of-a-different-panel-1, with no auto-cascade (cascading would burn budget without operator consent and might overwrite work they were happy with).

**Concurrency is an adaptive worker pool.** Phase 2 runs `K` concurrent workers pulling from a FIFO of `PanelTarget`s in story order; `K` starts at 3, decrements immediately on any 429 (floor 1), increments after 5 consecutive successes (ceiling 8). State is per-session — each new session starts fresh at K=3 because the upstream quota posture might be totally different on next launch. 429s use ADR-0003's existing [[Throttled]] semantics — single auto-retry per call, then surface as a countdown card if the call is the stack head, or invisible-but-retrying if it's mid-stack. Hard failures show a Failed card with three affordances: Retry, long-press = Re-prompt (the content-policy bounce recovery path), and a secondary **Defer** pill that advances the stack and leaves the panel's gallery empty. A comic with one or more Failed-Deferred panels finalizes after a confirm warning; `PanelGridCellStatus` extends from 4 to 5 states with a new `Failed` case. Skip — removed in slice 11a per the ADR-0003 amendment — is NOT reintroduced; Defer is the new vocabulary and is recoverable from the grid.

**Triptychs are reviewed as one super-card.** The P-in (panels 3–5) and H-out (panels 12–14) transition triptychs from ADR-0007 share one geometric composition in the print layout, and the review surface mirrors that: a single card renders all three sub-panels in the print-faithful triptych layout, and Accept / Re-roll / Re-prompt act atomically on all three. Re-prompt on a triptych edits a single shared addendum string appended to each sub-panel's assembled prompt — per-sub-panel `caption`/`emotion`/`position` stay intact. A triptych Re-roll or Re-prompt spends 3 calls against the per-comic budget. Reviewing the three sub-panels independently was rejected because the failure mode "accept 2/3, re-roll 1/3" silently damages the design (the bookends end up stylistically out-of-sync with the new middle), and the print layout treats them as one composition anyway. The grid escape hatch + auto-present-at-completion handles late-stage navigation: a toolbar grid button is always available; tapping an Accepted cell fires a Re-roll-after-accept confirm and jumps the stack back to that panel; when the last unaccepted panel reaches Accept, the grid auto-presents with the existing "Generate PDF" CTA as the natural finalize affordance.

The per-comic generation budget (ADR-0008) becomes **template-dynamic**: `(panel_count + 1) × 2`, currently 32 for the 15-panel template, but a future 9-panel trial template would scale to 20 calls automatically. `GenerationBudget.limit` parameterizes on slot count. The chip placement and copy may shift but the layered friction model (per-comic budget + per-install Sponsored trial + global GCP cap) is unchanged from ADR-0008. Exhaustion in the swipe surface disables swipe-left (Re-roll) and long-press (Re-prompt); swipe-right (Accept) and swipe-up/down (gallery cycle, 0 calls) remain. The exhaustion modal preserves slice 23's offer of "accept current and finalize" or "paste BYO key." Onboarding for the new gesture vocabulary is a one-time first-launch overlay (Tinder-style ghost-finger animation) re-summonable from Settings. The "Start campaign" entry point is a full-screen CTA after the QA gate, giving the operator a deliberate moment to commit (especially load-bearing under `.sponsored` where Start spends a comic from the trial counter) and hosting a brief two-phase model explanation.
