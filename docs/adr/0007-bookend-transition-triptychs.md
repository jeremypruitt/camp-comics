# Bookend transition triptychs replace the diagonal P10/P11 pair

The page-3 diagonal pair (ADR-0005) is superseded by two symmetric **transition triptychs** that bookend the quest — one on page 2 (transition INTO fantasy, **P-in**) and one on page 4 (transition BACK to everyday, **H-out**). Each triptych is three panels sharing one figure container with constant-width cream gaps along diagonal seams; the middle cell is a face-out-of-frame close-up on the prop, and the bookending panels frame the triptych narratively. Triptych panels emit no `<figcaption>` (Watchmen-style); the adjacent on-page panels carry the captions.

Panel count grows **12 → 15** to make room for two three-cell triptychs and the new act-2 introduction beat where the mentor hands the prop over. Act distribution is 6 / 5 / 4 across the three interior pages — Act I (panels 1–6, page 2) ends with the splash hero-shot just after the P-in triptych; Act II (panels 7–11, page 3) is the quest itself, now starting with the gift handoff at new-P8; Act III (panels 12–15, page 4) is the H-out triptych plus the kitchen return splash. The Cover is the 16th generated artifact. `PanelTarget` Int range extends to 1…15 and `PlayerStatus` totals auto-adjust via `template.panels.count + 1` — no code change beyond the YAML re-write and the renderer.

Geometry differs deliberately between the two triptychs. **P-in** (page 2) uses a parallelogram middle and trapezoid bookends, all slashes leaning //// (top-left to bottom-right) — forward-leaning motion matching the hero being pulled into the quest. **H-out** (page 4) uses a hexagonal diamond-middle and pentagon bookends — a ceremonial pause where the hex's mid-height widening centers the gift. Clip-paths are CSS `clip-path: polygon(...)` (no SVG wrapper), same approach as ADR-0005's geometry math: mirrored for the P-in slash direction, and extended to the hex+pentagon shapes for H-out. The constant 3.5pp gap from ADR-0005 carries over.

Triptych middles (new-P4 and new-P13) are **extreme close-ups on hand-and-prop with the face out of frame** — the "abstract-via-framing" pattern. This removes the face-fidelity risk that drove the failed centroid-targeting work (PR #37, superseded with this ADR): there is no full hero figure to crop badly because there is no full hero figure in the middle cell at all. Triptych middles use only `scene:` + `composition:` in their YAML; no `style_override:` or `reference_panel:` overrides are needed because STYLE_SUFFIX's face-fidelity language doesn't fight close-up framing of just a hand.

The prop is the **visual through-line**. The mentor hands it over glowing at new-P8 (act-2 introduction — relocated from the legacy farewell beat), it carries through the quest, appears glowing again in the H-out left bookend (new-P12), visibly dims in the H-out middle close-up (new-P13), and lands mundane in the right bookend (new-P14) and the kitchen return splash (new-P15). Each class's prop is the same one already defined in its current OLD-P12 entry; the YAML rewrite ports those props forward into their new homes.

The alternative considered was keeping the single page-3 diagonal pair and improving placement via geometric centroid math (Apple Vision saliency, PR #37). It worked technically but failed visually — the centroid was the wrong target for "where the hero should sit in the trapezoid." Replacing the pair with a triptych that uses hand-only middles removes the failure mode by removing the constraint: when there's no full hero figure in the abstract panels, there's no figure to crop badly. The redesign touches every class YAML, the renderer, the CSS, and the act-split math, so it earns its own ADR even though it shares the underlying clip-path technique with ADR-0005.
